Cycling of Materials in Ecosystems

Understanding how nature recycles water, carbon, and nutrients

Created by: Mary Magdaline Barrun

About the Ecosystem Cycle

Cycling of materials in ecosystems refers to the continuous movement of nutrients and substances through living organisms and the environment. These cycles help maintain balance in nature and support life on Earth.

Carbon Cycle

The carbon cycle explains how carbon moves between the atmosphere, plants, animals, and soil. Plants absorb carbon dioxide during photosynthesis while animals release it through respiration.

Water Cycle

The water cycle includes evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection. It continuously recycles water throughout Earth’s atmosphere and surface.

Nitrogen Cycle

The nitrogen cycle shows how nitrogen moves through the soil, plants, animals, and atmosphere. It is essential for plant growth and protein production.
